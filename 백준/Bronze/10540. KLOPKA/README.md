### 문제 설명

<p>The city of Osijek has recently been plagued by a swarm of mosquitoes. The solution to this problem was proposed long ago by Mr. Perić, a brave inventor from Benkovci, in an episode of the TV-show Gitak called &quot;Globalno sjelo&quot;<sup>1</sup>. Among other &nbsp;inspiring inventions, he presented a mosquito trap. It is basically a box with which you cover the mosquito after it falls for the &nbsp;piece of cheese or &quot;kajmak&quot; you placed there, depending on what your mosquitoes prefer. Simple, isn&#39;t it?&nbsp;</p>

<p>If you&#39;re lucky, the box can cover more than one mosquito. You have spotted N mosquitoes on the table and know their &nbsp;positions precisely. What is the area of the smallest square-shaped box that can, placed parallel to the sides of the &nbsp;table, cover all the mosquitoes? The box, of course, can cover the mosquito with its edge.&nbsp;</p>

<p><sup>1</sup><a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mutagnjp_VA">http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mutagnjp_VA</a></p>

### 입력

<p>The first line of input contains the integer N (2 &le; N &le; 20), the number of spotted mosquitoes.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Each of the following N lines contains the positions of mosquitoes as space-separated integer coordinates X and Y (1 &le; X, Y &le; 100) in an imaginary coordinate system whose axes are the sides of the table. At least two mosquitoes will be in different &nbsp;positions.&nbsp;</p>

### 출력

<p>The first and only line of output must contain the required area of the smallest square-shaped box (expressed, of course, in unit squares of the aforementioned coordinate system).&nbsp;</p>

### 힌트

<p>Clarification of the first sample test: A square with vertices (3,3) and (7,7) solves all the problems.</p>
