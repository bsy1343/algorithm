<p>We&rsquo;re all familiar with harshad numbers. For this problem, you will ... what&rsquo;s that? You aren&rsquo;t familiar with harshad numbers? They&rsquo;re also known as Niven numbers &ndash; does that ring a bell?? Anything???</p>

<p>Well, it&rsquo;s a simple enough concept. A harshad number is a number which is evenly divisible by the sum of its digits. For example, 24 is a harshad number: the sum of its digits is 2 + 4 = 6 and 24 is divisible by 6. 156 is also a harshad number, since 1 + 5 + 6 = 12 and 156 = (12)(13). 157 is NOT a harshad number since it is not divisible by 1 + 5 + 7 = 13.</p>
