### 문제 설명

<p>Everyone knows that the letter &ldquo;E&rdquo; is the most frequent letter in the English language. In fact, there are one hundred sixteen E&rsquo;s on this very page ... no, make that one hundred twenty one. Indeed, when spelling out integers it is interesting to see which ones do NOT use the letter &ldquo;E&rdquo;. For example 6030 (six thousand thirty) doesn&rsquo;t. Nor does 4002064 (four million two thousand sixty four).</p>

<p>It turns out that 6030 is the 64th positive integer that does not use an &ldquo;E&rdquo; when spelled out and 4002064 is the 838th such number. Your job is to find the n-th such number.</p>

<p>Note: 1,001,001,001,001,001,001,001,001,000 is &ldquo;one octillion, one septillion, one sextillion, one quintillion, one quadrillion, one trillion, one billion, one million, one thousand&rdquo;. (Whew!)</p>

### 입력

<p>The input file will consist of multiple test cases. Each input case will consist of one positive integer n (less than 2<sup>31</sup>) on a line. A 0 indicates end-of-input. (There will be no commas in the input.)</p>

### 출력

<p>For each input n you will print, with appropriate commas, the n-th positive integer whose spelling does not use an &ldquo;E&rdquo;. You may assume that all answers are less than 10<sup>28</sup>.</p>
