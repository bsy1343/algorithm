### 문제 설명

<p>Hercule Poirot, world-renowned detective, was having a lovely cup of tea in his compartment on the Disorient Express when the train conductor rushed in. &quot;We have lost track of the number of train carriages,&quot; exclaimed the conductor. You see, this was no ordinary train, and had no first or last train carriage. Instead, the train carriages were connected to form a large cycle, causing the conductor&#39;s confusion.</p>

<p>Hercule thought for a moment. &quot;That is most peculiar,&quot; he said. &quot;But I may be able to help you.&quot; He reached over and grabbed the lamp from the side table. &quot;You see, each train carriage has a light switch like this one. By moving between the carriages and toggling these switches, we can determine the number of train carriages.&quot;</p>

<p>The conductor was sceptical, but agreed to try it. &quot;We are in a hurry,&quot; he said, &quot;so please determine $n$, the number of carriages that the train consists of, in at most $3n+500$ steps.&quot; Here a step counts as either moving to an adjacent carriage or toggling a light switch in the current carriage. &quot;The only thing I am certain of is that $n$ is at least $3$ and at most $5000$.&quot;</p>
